GDPR Statement

Human Integrity Advisory

Our commitments under UK GDPR and how we handle personal data in the context of independent advisory services.

Last updated: January 2025. This statement sets out HIA's approach to data protection under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

01 Our commitment

Human Integrity Advisory is committed to handling personal data responsibly, transparently, and in full compliance with UK data protection law. This is not a compliance posture. It is consistent with the values on which this practice is built.

We process the minimum personal data necessary for our purposes, we do not retain it beyond what is required, and we do not use it for purposes other than those for which it was collected. We apply the same standard of integrity to data handling that we apply to advisory work.

02 Data controller

Human Integrity Advisory is the data controller for all personal data collected through this website and through direct correspondence. As data controller, we are responsible for determining the purposes and means of processing personal data and for ensuring that processing is lawful, fair, and transparent.

Contact: advisory@hia.io

03 Lawful basis for processing

We process personal data on the following lawful bases under Article 6 UK GDPR:

  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) - for the evaluation and management of enquiries from prospective clients. Our legitimate interest is the operation of a professional advisory practice. We have assessed that this interest is not overridden by the rights and interests of the individuals concerned, given the professional context of all enquiries and the minimal data involved.
  • Performance of a contract (Article 6(1)(b)) - for the processing of personal data necessary to deliver advisory services where an engagement agreement has been entered into.
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)) - where processing is required to comply with a legal obligation applicable to us, including tax, accounting, and regulatory requirements.

We do not rely on consent as a lawful basis for processing in the ordinary course of our operations. We do not process special category personal data.

04 Data minimisation

We collect only the personal data that is necessary for the purpose for which it is collected. We do not request information we do not need. We do not retain personal data beyond the period for which it is required.

  • Website enquiry data is limited to: name, professional role, organisation, email address, and the context provided by the individual.
  • Engagement data is limited to what is necessary to scope, deliver, and document the advisory work agreed between the parties.
  • We do not collect sensitive personal data, financial data, or information about third parties beyond what is voluntarily and relevantly provided in the context of an enquiry or engagement.
05 Storage & security

Personal data is stored securely with access limited to those who need it for the purposes described in this statement. We use encrypted communication for correspondence and apply appropriate access controls to stored data.

  • Website enquiry forms are processed via Formspree, whose infrastructure is based in the United States. Data is transferred under appropriate safeguards in accordance with UK GDPR requirements for international transfers.
  • Correspondence is stored on encrypted, access-controlled systems.
  • We do not store personal data on unencrypted portable devices.

We review our data storage practices regularly and update them as required by changes in our operations or applicable guidance.

06 Retention periods
  • Enquiries not leading to an engagement - deleted within 12 months of the enquiry date.
  • Engagement records - retained for six years from the end of the engagement, in accordance with standard professional and legal requirements, then securely deleted.
  • Financial records - retained for seven years in accordance with HMRC requirements.

When a retention period expires, personal data is securely and permanently deleted or anonymised so that it can no longer be attributed to an individual.

07 Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights in relation to personal data we hold about you. We will respond to all valid requests within one calendar month.

Right of access

You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a Subject Access Request). We will provide this free of charge unless the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

Right to rectification

You may request that inaccurate or incomplete personal data be corrected without undue delay.

Right to erasure

You may request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, or where you have objected to processing and there is no overriding legitimate ground to continue. This right is subject to any legal obligations that require us to retain data.

Right to restriction

You may request that we restrict processing of your personal data in certain circumstances - for example, while the accuracy of data is being contested, or while an objection is being assessed.

Right to object

You may object to processing carried out on the basis of legitimate interests. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or unless processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.

Right to data portability

Where processing is carried out on the basis of contract or consent, and by automated means, you may request that personal data you have provided to us be transmitted to you or to another controller in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

08 How to exercise your rights

To exercise any of the rights described above, or to raise a concern about how we handle your personal data, contact us at:

advisory@hia.io

We will acknowledge your request within five working days and respond in full within one calendar month. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing a request.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection matters, at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

09 Automated decision-making

HIA does not carry out automated decision-making or profiling in relation to the personal data it processes. All decisions relating to enquiries and engagements are made by senior advisors exercising human judgement.

10 Changes to this statement

We review this statement regularly and update it as required by changes to our operations, to applicable law, or to ICO guidance. The date at the top of this page indicates when it was last revised.

For questions about this statement or any aspect of our data protection practices, contact us at advisory@hia.io.
