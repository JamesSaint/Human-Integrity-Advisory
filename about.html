Independent Intervention Advisory

About HIA

An independent advisory practice assessing whether organisations can intervene in their AI systems when it matters. Grounded in operational experience, not governance theory.

00Founder

James Saint

Founder

James Saint is the founder of Human Integrity Advisory. His background is in applied AI systems within financial intelligence environments: operational exposure to how AI-driven decisions are made, contested, and escalated in high-consequence contexts.

HIA's technical grounding is informed by collaborative work with Professor Nicholas Ryman-Tubb (University of Surrey) on neural network explainability. The combination of operational experience and rigorous academic foundation is what separates HIA from advisory built on frameworks alone.

01Why HIA exists

Most governance structures were designed to describe what should happen. They were not designed to survive contact with a live system event.

When information is incomplete, time is short, and authority structures were not built for the moment at hand, governance frameworks rarely determine what will happen. HIA exists in that gap.

The moment governance is tested is where this work begins.

This is not ethics consulting. It is not AI safety philosophy. It is a practical assessment of whether your organisation can intervene in its own AI systems when the situation demands it.

If you are looking for reassurance, HIA is not the right advisor. If you want an accurate account of your organisation's intervention capability, and are prepared to act on it, we should talk.
02Experience & grounding

HIA's assessments are grounded in direct experience with AI systems as they operate in practice, not as they are described in documentation. Real systems, real outputs, real decisions under pressure.

That includes work in Financial Intelligence & Technical Systems (FITS) environments: real-time decision requirements, significant regulatory exposure, and material human cost when intervention fails. These conditions expose precisely the failure modes HIA now assesses.

The technical foundation comes from collaborative research with Professor Nicholas Ryman-Tubb (University of Surrey) on neural network explainability for payment card fraud detection. This is a domain where model behaviour, regulatory scrutiny, and human consequence converge with no margin for abstraction.

Technical fluency is not the goal. It is what keeps the assessment anchored in how systems actually behave, not how they are described.

That grounding informs AGDA, HIA's proprietary diagnostic architecture for assessing intervention capability. AGDA exposes the gap between declared oversight and real operational control under pressure.

03Independence

HIA is not affiliated with any technology vendor, governance body, regulatory authority, or advisory network. Our assessments are not shaped by vendor relationships or by the commercial dynamics that govern how most advisory work is commissioned.

This matters because the question HIA answers, whether your organisation can intervene in time, requires an assessor whose interests are aligned with an accurate answer. Not a reassuring one. Not a commercially convenient one.

Where HIA works alongside specialist governance firms, including Dr. Joanna Michalska of Ethica Group (ethics frameworks and board-level governance design), we do so as a separate, independent voice. The governance layer and the intervention layer must be assessed independently to have any value.

04What this brings to your organisation
  • Independent assessment of intervention capability, not shaped by those who designed your governance framework.
  • Practitioner-level understanding of AI system behaviour in operational environments. Not a compliance checklist.
  • Accurate translation between engineering, legal, risk, and executive domains without distortion.
  • A clear account of where your decision architecture fails, before a system event makes that account unavoidable.
  • Structured diagnostic evidence produced through AGDA (HIA's proprietary assessment architecture). Not a maturity checklist.
The throughline is precision. We tell you what is real about your organisation's intervention capability, not what is reassuring about your governance framework.

06How we work

Assessment before advice

Every engagement begins with structured assessment. We do not arrive with pre-formed recommendations. We determine what is actually true about your intervention capability before we advise on it.

Practitioner perspective

Assessments are conducted by practitioners who have worked inside complex AI systems environments. We evaluate governance against operational reality, not against a framework.

Accuracy over assurance

We report what is accurate, not what is comfortable. Every finding is evidenced and defensible.

Limited engagements

HIA works with a limited number of organisations at any time. Each engagement receives full senior attention. This is not a volume practice.

07If you are considering working together

If you hold responsibility for AI governance or board oversight of AI-dependent operations, the first step is a discovery call. A short, confidential conversation to establish context and determine whether an engagement is a fit.
