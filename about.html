About

Why HIA exists

HIA exists because the most dangerous failures are often visible only after the organisation has already lost the ability to intervene.

00Founder

James Saint

Founder

James Saint is the founder of Human Integrity Advisory. His background is in applied AI systems within financial intelligence environments: operational exposure to how AI-driven decisions are made, contested, and escalated in high-consequence contexts.

HIA's technical grounding is informed by collaborative work with Professor Nicholas Ryman-Tubb (University of Surrey) on neural network explainability. The combination of operational experience and rigorous academic foundation is what separates HIA from advisory built on frameworks alone.

01Why HIA exists

HIA is an independent integrity advisory for high-stakes decisions.

We work with leaders responsible for systems where the gap between governance design and operational reality is not academic. Where the cost of discovering that gap in real time is not recoverable.

Our work begins where governance design ends. The moment control is tested against reality.

This is not ethics consulting. It is not AI safety philosophy. It is the practical assessment of whether your organisation can intervene in its own systems before a decision becomes irreversible.

HIA does not provide reassurance. We provide an accurate account of whether your organisation can act when it needs to.
02Founder

James Saint

Founder

HIA is founded by James Saint. The advisory is grounded in applied experience with AI systems in financial intelligence and transaction surveillance environments: operational exposure to how high-consequence decisions are made, contested, escalated, and overridden under real conditions.

That experience produced a specific insight. Governance frameworks are designed for control, but most organisations have never tested whether that control actually works when a system fails at speed. HIA exists to close that gap.

Technical foundation

HIA's technical methodology is informed by collaboration with Professor Nicholas Ryman-Tubb (University of Surrey) on neural network explainability and the conditions under which human decision-makers can meaningfully oversee automated systems.

Independence

HIA operates independently of all technology vendors, governance bodies, and advisory networks. Where relevant, HIA works alongside specialist governance firms as a separate, independent voice. The advisory relationship is with the accuracy of the finding, not with the interests that commission most advisory work.

03What we stand for

Independent judgement

No vendor affiliations. No framework allegiances. No commercial pressure to shape findings. Our interests are aligned with an accurate answer, not a reassuring one.

Pattern recognition

System failure rarely announces itself. We work upstream of visible crisis, where the signals are still subtle and the cost of acting is still low.

Clarity over complexity

A board that cannot act on the finding cannot govern the system. We write what the decision owner needs, not what the framework requires.

Refusal of false control

Governance documents are not operational control. The distinction matters. We refuse to mistake the first for the second.

04Proprietary method

HIA's assessment engine is AGDA, a proprietary system for testing intervention viability under real-world conditions of time, dependency, evidence, and control.

AGDA is not a product. It is how we see. It is how findings are grounded in something more than opinion and more than framework.
