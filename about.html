About James Saint

Founder · Human Integrity Advisory

I help boards and senior leaders see clearly where AI systems, organisational power, and human reality intersect — so that high-stakes decisions do not quietly cause harm.

My work is built on two anchors: authenticity and integrity.
They are not values I market. They are conditions I work inside, or I do not work.

01 Why this work exists

Human Integrity Advisory is the result of years spent inside technical, institutional, and human systems that did not always match the way they described themselves. I have seen how decisions are shaped by pressure, hierarchy, gaps in information, and the desire to appear certain. I have also seen how those same decisions are lived by the people who carry their consequences.

My work exists for leaders who want clarity rather than performance. I sit at the meeting point of two realities. One is the formal governance and technical structure behind decisions. The other is the lived nervous system that absorbs them. When those realities drift apart, harm accumulates quietly and out of view. Integrity is the work of bringing them back into alignment.

This is not abstract for me. I have lived the cost of misalignment. That is what keeps my attention steady, unflinching, and grounded in what is real.

02 Human integrity as lived expertise

Many organisations treat ethics as a policy surface. My work sits closer to the point where decisions land. I understand what it is to be downstream of institutional judgement, and that lived experience shapes my approach. It does not make the work emotional. It makes it honest. It prevents me from being captured by the performance of ethics rather than its substance.

This perspective gives me a habit of listening for what is not said, what is implied, or what is quietly handed to people with less power. Integrity is often a question of attention. Where it goes. Where it does not. What is allowed to be named. What is quietly avoided.

Lived experience does not replace analytical discipline. It strengthens it. It removes distortion.

03 Technical & systems foundations

Before founding Human Integrity Advisory I worked across applied AI, fraud detection, and full-stack development. Technical fluency is not my identity, but it is part of my foundation. It means I can interrogate assumptions, spot pressure points, and translate accurately between engineers, product teams, legal, and leadership without distortion.

  • Neural network behaviour and the trade-offs inside real-world fraud systems.
  • Full-stack development work bridging design, engineering, governance, and human context.
  • First-hand experience of commercial pressure, compliance requirements, and the human cost embedded within technical decisions.

Technical fluency is not the goal. It is what keeps the conversation anchored in reality rather than organisational aspiration.

04 AI, fraud systems & explainability

Between 2014 and 2020 I worked closely with Dr (now Professor) Nicholas F. Ryman-Tubb at the University of Surrey on neural network explainability for payment card fraud. I helped develop the structural backbone for the contextual and literature foundations of his doctoral work and designed the conceptual diagrams and frameworks used throughout.

That work exposed me to the real tension points inside AI systems. Thresholds. Error trade-offs. Narrative pressure. Regulatory scrutiny. Human consequence. It taught me how models behave outside controlled academic environments and how organisations respond when uncertainty becomes uncomfortable.

Explainability is not documentation. It is a governance requirement and a human requirement.

05 FITS, FATHOM™ & lessons in ownership

A significant part of my trajectory came through co-founding the Institute of Financial Innovation in Transactions and Security (FITS). FITS was designed as a not-for-profit response to payment card fraud and the movement of money into criminal economies, supported by the University of Surrey and European grant funding.

Although my title began as Chief Marketing Officer, the operational and product load moved toward me. I became the de facto Chief Operating Officer and product designer — building the public-facing ecosystem, creating the technical infrastructure, and translating research into something partners could actually use.

The primary output was FATHOM™, a real-time fraud management platform built on Intenda fraXses data architecture. I designed and built the full front-end interface that investigators, analysts, and executives worked inside.

  • Turning research and neural network outputs into decision-ready interfaces.
  • Designing the full front end over Intenda fraXses data architecture.
  • Coordinating academics, engineers, and institutional partners from concept to product.
  • Protecting the original societal mission and resisting extraction pressure.

When FATHOM™ reached commercial value, the intellectual property was sold. The experience revealed how structures can quietly erase the people who build the foundations. Human Integrity Advisory is a continuation of that trajectory — but with different boundaries.

06 What I bring to your organisation

Human Integrity Advisory exists so leaders have a single independent counterpart who can hold the whole landscape — technical, human, commercial, and ethical. My work is to show you what your systems and decisions are actually doing and to help you act with clarity rather than fear or performance.

  • Independent evaluation of AI and data-driven systems where decisions carry human or legal risk.
  • Accurate translation between engineering, legal, product, and executive domains.
  • Design and refinement of integrity and escalation frameworks that can be lived rather than published.
  • Support for leaders navigating complex, politically sensitive, or emotionally loaded decisions.

The throughline is simple. I help you see reality clearly enough to act with both courage and conscience.

07 How this shows up in the room

I do not perform expertise. I hold clarity. In practice that means:

  • Listening for contradictions, pressure points, and risks others have normalised.
  • Translating complexity into language that leaders can stand behind without doubt or inflation.
  • Naming misalignment directly and respectfully so it can be acted on.
  • Leaving you with written clarity and grounded next steps.

My standard is this: decisions you could explain with a clear conscience to anyone affected by them.
