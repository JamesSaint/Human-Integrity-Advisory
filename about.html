Professional Overview

My work centres on integrity in environments where technology, strategy, regulation, and human lives meet. I advise boards, founders, and senior leaders facing decisions that have ethical, reputational, or human impact. My perspective is grounded in practical AI exposure, systems thinking, and a clear understanding of how institutional decisions land in real-world contexts.

I provide a direct, analytical, and confidential perspective for leadership teams who want decisions they can justify publicly, privately, and ethically. My focus is always the same: clarity, coherence, and alignment between intention, system behaviour, and lived human experience.

Early Foundations in Applied AI

Between 2014 and 2020, I worked closely with Dr (now Professor) Nicholas F. Ryman-Tubb during the development of his PhD at the University of Surrey: Understanding Payment Card Fraud Through Knowledge Extraction from Neural Networks Using Large-Scale Datasets.

While formally credited for illustration support, my involvement provided substantial exposure to neural-symbolic reasoning, large-scale data environments, AI explainability challenges, and fraud detection systems. This period acted as a deep practical foundation that complemented my existing full-stack development expertise across HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, MySQL, Python, Java, C++, and R.

Co-Founder and COO, FITS

Building on that early AI work, I co-founded FITS – Financial Innovation in Transactions & Security, a non-profit initiative designed to bridge applied AI research with industry to reduce global fraud. Dr Ryman-Tubb served as CEO; I held the role of COO.

I was responsible for establishing the full external and operational identity of the organisation, including:

  • Brand identity and logo creation
  • Website development and digital infrastructure
  • Commercial brochure and marketing collateral
  • Email systems, hosting, and operational setup
  • LinkedIn and public-facing communications
  • Stakeholder alignment and organisational positioning

This work enabled FITS to engage credibly with financial institutions, regulators, research partners, and industry stakeholders across fraud and cyber risk.

Current Focus

Today, I bring this combined technical and organisational foundation into independent advisory work. My focus is helping leadership teams reduce ethical blind spots, evaluate AI and data-driven systems, and make decisions that are strategically sound, ethically grounded, and publicly defensible.

I work directly with boards, founders, and executives on:

  • Integrity advisory in boardroom decision environments
  • AI and systems ethics review with attention to human impact
  • Executive reflection sessions on power, responsibility, and judgement

The aim is consistent: decisions that align with reality, withstand scrutiny, and protect trust.
