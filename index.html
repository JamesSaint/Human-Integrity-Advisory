01Where the chain breaks

Systems do not fail at the decision. They fail before a decision is possible.

Signal breaks. Escalation stalls. Dependency obscures authority. Time runs out. By the moment a decision could have been made, the window has closed.

Governance only matters if action happens before irreversibility. HIA exists to test whether it can.

What we use to see this.

HIA uses a proprietary assessment engine to test whether intervention is actually possible under real-world conditions of time, dependency, evidence, and control.

04How would you know you need this

You do not have a defined problem. You have a set of conditions a board member or CRO would recognise immediately.

Your governance framework is documented. You are not certain what would happen if a live system produced an outcome requiring an urgent, board-level response. You cannot name, precisely, the first person with authority to act, or whether they could act at the speed the situation demands.

You have not tested it. Not because of negligence. Because the structures that would need to be tested were never designed for that pressure.

If any of this is recognisable

  • Your governance framework is in place but has never been stress-tested to confirm it produces action, not just assurance.
  • Your escalation chain exists on paper but has never been traced from system event to human decision under time pressure.
  • You cannot name, right now, the person who would halt a live AI-driven decision.
  • You are responsible for oversight but have no independent view of whether the organisation can actually intervene.
The gap between governance design and operational capability is structural. Better documentation does not close it.
