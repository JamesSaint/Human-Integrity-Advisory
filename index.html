01The failure we address

Governance tells your organisation what should happen. HIA determines whether it can.

Every regulated organisation running consequential AI has a governance framework. Policies are written. Escalation paths are documented. Oversight committees exist. None of this answers the question that matters: when a live system produces an outcome that demands intervention, can your organisation deliver one?

Not in theory. Not according to the framework. In practice, under real time pressure, with the people and authority structures you actually have. That is the question HIA exists to answer.

We focus on the systems that matter most, under the conditions where organisations are least likely to be able to stop them.

  • Whether your escalation paths function under real operational conditions, not just on paper.
  • Whether the person with authority to halt a live AI decision can be reached, informed, and empowered to act within the window that matters.
  • Whether your board would know it needed to intervene before the cost of not doing so became uncontainable.
AGDA, HIA's proprietary diagnostic architecture, is how this gap is assessed and evidenced in a form the board can act on directly.

03How we select use cases

We focus on the systems that matter most, under the conditions where organisations are least likely to be able to stop them.

Not every AI system in your organisation carries the same intervention risk. HIA identifies the use cases where failure compounds fastest, where authority is most ambiguous, and where the gap between governance design and operational capability is widest.

The assessment begins with these systems. Everything that follows is calibrated to the specific risk profile they present.

Selection criteria

  • Systems where a failure in real time would produce regulatory, financial, or reputational consequences that compound by the hour.
  • Use cases where the escalation path from system event to human authority is longest or most ambiguous.
  • Decisions where the person with override authority cannot currently be reached, informed, and empowered to act within the window that matters.
  • Systems where the governance framework has never been tested against the operational conditions it was designed for.

05How would you know you need this?

Most organisations that need HIA do not have a defined problem. They have a set of conditions that a board member or CRO would recognise immediately.

Your AI governance framework has been documented, reviewed, and approved. You are not certain what would happen if a live system produced an outcome that required an urgent, board-level response. You do not know, precisely, who would be the first person with authority to act, how long it would take them to be informed, or whether they could act at the speed the situation demands.

You have not tested it. Not because of negligence. Because the structures that would need to be tested were never designed for that kind of pressure.

If any of this is recognisable

  • You have a governance framework in place but have never stress-tested whether it produces action, not just assurance.
  • Your escalation chain exists on paper but has never been traced from system event to human decision under time pressure.
  • You cannot name, right now, the person who would halt a live AI decision, or how long it would take to reach them.
  • You are responsible for AI oversight but have no independent view of whether the organisation can actually intervene.
The gap between governance design and operational capability is structural. It is not resolved by better documentation. A conversation costs nothing and will tell you whether an HIA engagement is relevant.

08How engagement works

Five stages. Each one scoped, contained, and designed to produce a clear result before the next begins. No open-ended retainers. No ambiguous deliverables.

Stage 1: Discovery call

A confidential conversation with relevant senior leadership. No questionnaire. No preparatory documents. We establish context, identify the systems that matter, and determine whether there is a fit.

Stage 2: Pre-qualification

HIA confirms the engagement is within scope: the organisation has consequential AI systems, the leadership is prepared to act on honest findings, and the question is real. Not every enquiry results in an engagement. That selectivity is deliberate.

Stage 3: Executive Exposure Assessment

AGDA, HIA's proprietary diagnostic architecture, structures the full assessment: mapping escalation integrity, authority clarity, and intervention viability as they actually exist. Not as governance documents describe them. Findings are presented directly to the board.

Stage 4: Executive Intervention Lab

Scenario-based pressure testing against the use cases identified in the Exposure Assessment. Your leadership team, your authority structures, real conditions. Calibrated to the specific vulnerability profile AGDA reveals.

Stage 5: Expansion and programme

For organisations that have moved past assessment and are building durable intervention capability. Ongoing advisory, cross-functional rollout, and enterprise-level control architecture. Scoped separately in writing.

HIA works with a deliberately small number of organisations at any one time. That is a constraint, not a growth ambition. Each engagement receives the full attention of senior advisors with direct operational experience of AI systems.

Based in the United Kingdom. Working with clients internationally through remote advisory and selected in-person sessions.
