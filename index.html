Why organisations work with me

Most organisations have values on the wall and a risk register on the server. Fewer have a clear view of what really happens in the room when money, power, technology, and human lives meet under pressure.

I work with boards and senior leadership teams who want clarity, not theatre. My focus is human integrity in practice: how decisions are made and justified, how AI and data systems are designed and deployed, and how all of this lands in the nervous systems and daily lives of real people.

My background sits at the intersection of systems thinking, explainable AI, and lived experience of both institutional harm and repair. I am not a brand story on a slide. I am an independent mind in the room who will name what your people and your customers will feel but may never say.

Outcomes you can expect

  • Clearer framing of complex ethical and strategic decisions.
  • Reduced risk of reputational, regulatory, and human harm.
  • Stronger, more coherent narratives you can stand behind publicly and internally.
  • Actionable next steps that your existing teams can realistically implement.

All advisory work is confidential and off record unless explicitly agreed otherwise.

What I do

Most of my work sits in moments where decisions carry weight, where AI, data, culture, or public trust are in play and leaders need clarity they cannot get internally. The formats below reflect how organisations use me when they need an independent mind who can see the technical, ethical, and human layers at once.

1. Boardroom Integrity Advisory

A confidential, unvarnished perspective in the room when decisions matter. I listen to the language, omissions, and pressure points, then translate what is happening, not just what is being said into clear risks, opportunities, and integrity thresholds. The work is direct, practical, and free of performance.

  • Support for sensitive or high pressure decisions.
  • Real time clarity when alignment is fractured or truth is hard to name.
  • Integrity framing leaders can stand behind publicly and internally.
  • Written synthesis capturing risks, thresholds, and next steps.

Used during restructures, crises, contested decisions, or moments of heightened scrutiny.

2. AI and Systems Ethics Review

Many AI ethics programmes sit at the level of policy. My work begins with the human experience of the system. I examine how your models, workflows, and decision rules feel to the person with the least power in contact with them, then map the points where harm is most likely to accumulate. The output is a clear, actionable set of recommendations for leadership, legal, and technical teams.

  • Independent review of AI, data, and decision systems through the lens of human impact.
  • Assessment of explainability, uncertainty, thresholds, and assumptions.
  • Identification of risk concentration and silent failure modes.
  • Prioritised recommendations leaders can act on immediately.

Used when launching or scaling AI products, preparing for regulatory scrutiny, or needing a grounded view of fairness and risk.

3. Executive Reflection and Integrity Labs

Leaders do not need another framework. They need somewhere they can think honestly without performance. I work one to one with CEOs and senior teams to examine blind spots, power dynamics, integrity risks, and the narratives shaping their decisions. The work is structured, calm, and anchored in real organisational pressures.

  • One to one reflection sessions with CEOs and senior leaders.
  • Small group Integrity Labs exploring culture, language, and decision dynamics.
  • Honest naming of patterns leaders cannot see from inside the hierarchy.
  • Written synthesis to support behavioural, cultural, and strategic alignment.

Used when decisions feel heavy, culture is strained, or leaders need honest, contained reflection.

Typical engagements and fees

My work is structured into defined formats so that scope, expectations, and boundaries are clear from the outset. Fees reflect both direct engagement and the preparation, analysis, and integrity framing required to hold this work to a high standard. I do not offer informal advice, off-the-record guidance, or unscoped support outside the agreements below.

  • Integrity Retainer
    A named independent advisor for boards, founders, or senior teams. Includes scheduled calls, written synthesis, document review, and advisory presence around key decisions within the agreed scope.
    Typical range: £2,000 to £4,000 per month.

Used when leaders want stable, ongoing clarity and an independent mind tracking integrity across decisions.

  • Ethics Review Sprint
    A focused two to three week engagement examining an AI, data, or decision system through the lens of human impact, explainability, and risk concentration. Includes a concise, prioritised set of recommendations for leadership, legal, and technical teams.
    Typical range: £4,000 to £6,000.

Used when launching, scaling, or reassessing systems that affect people’s lives, rights, or outcomes.

  • Executive Reflection Session
    A confidential 90-minute session for a CEO or senior leader navigating a complex or contested decision. Includes a written synthesis outlining risks, patterns, and options.
    Typical range: £750 to £1,000.

Used when a leader needs clear sight, not performance, and a grounded space to think.

Working boundaries

All work is pre-scoped in writing. There is no informal support outside the agreed structure. Additional questions, calls, or document reviews fall outside scope and are treated as new pieces of work with their own fee and written agreement. This protects the integrity of the work and ensures decisions are supported with the discipline and clarity they require.

How I work

My work is grounded, confidential, and deliberately free from performance. I am not a trainer or a keynote speaker. I am a long view ally whose only stake is the integrity of the work and the lives it touches.

  • Focused advisory for a defined decision, product, or review period.
  • Quarterly board relationship as a named independent integrity advisor.
  • Executive reflection retainers for ongoing one to one work with key leaders.

You can expect direct conversation, careful listening, and written follow ups that make next steps obvious without adding cosmetic complexity.

What I am not

  • I am not a box ticking compliance consultant.
  • I am not a public relations shield for decisions that are already misaligned.
  • I am not available to justify what you already know is indefensible.

Questions I help boards hold

  • Where does our current AI or data strategy quietly contradict the values we say we stand for.
  • What are we asking our most vulnerable users or customers to absorb on our behalf.
  • What would it look like for this decision to be one we are still proud of in ten years time.
  • What happens to trust if our next system failure becomes public before we have spoken about it honestly.

From there we move from questions to a concrete plan: specific risks, clear trade offs, and pragmatic steps that respect regulatory reality, commercial constraints, and human lives together.
