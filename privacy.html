Privacy Policy

Human Integrity Advisory

How we collect, hold, and protect information provided to us through this website and through direct correspondence.

Last updated: January 2025. This policy applies to Human Integrity Advisory and all information collected through this website or through direct contact with us.

01 Who we are

Human Integrity Advisory ("HIA", "we", "us") is an independent advisory practice operating in the United Kingdom. For the purposes of UK data protection law, HIA is the data controller of personal information collected through this website and through direct correspondence.

If you have any questions about this policy or how we handle your personal information, contact us at advisory@hia.io.

02 Information we collect

We collect only the information you choose to provide. This includes:

  • Enquiry information - your name, professional role, organisation, email address, and the context you provide when making an enquiry through the website form or by direct email.
  • Correspondence - the content of any email or written communication you send to us.
  • Engagement information - if an advisory engagement proceeds, additional professional information relevant to the scope of the work.

We do not use tracking cookies, advertising technologies, or third-party analytics platforms on this website. We do not collect information about your browsing behaviour or device beyond what is standard in server logs.

03 How we use your information

Information you provide is used solely for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiry and determine whether an engagement is appropriate.
  • To conduct advisory work where an engagement proceeds, within the scope agreed in writing.
  • To comply with any legal or regulatory obligations applicable to us.

We do not use your information for marketing, profiling, or any purpose beyond the above. We do not send unsolicited communications.

Our legal basis for processing enquiry information is legitimate interest - specifically, the management of a professional advisory practice and the evaluation of potential client relationships. Where an engagement proceeds, processing is necessary for the performance of a contract.

04 Confidentiality

All information shared with HIA is treated with professional confidence. This applies equally to website enquiries and to information disclosed during advisory engagements. We operate to the same standard of discretion we require of others.

We do not discuss, reference, or disclose information about enquiries or engagements - including the fact that an enquiry or engagement has taken place - unless explicitly agreed otherwise in writing.

05 Sharing your information

We do not sell, share, or transfer your personal information to third parties, except in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers - we use Formspree to receive website form submissions. Information submitted through the form is transmitted to Formspree's servers in accordance with their privacy policy before being delivered to us. No other third-party services process your data on our behalf.
  • Legal requirements - we may disclose information where required to do so by law, court order, or regulatory authority.

We do not use your information to train AI models or share it with any AI system provider.

06 How long we keep your information
  • Enquiries that do not lead to an engagement - retained for up to 12 months from the date of the enquiry, then securely deleted.
  • Engagement information - retained for the duration of the engagement and for a period of six years thereafter, in accordance with standard professional practice and applicable legal requirements.
  • Direct correspondence - retained as long as it is relevant to an ongoing or potential engagement, and for a reasonable period thereafter.
07 Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the following rights in relation to personal information we hold about you:

  • Access - the right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction - the right to request that inaccurate or incomplete information be corrected.
  • Erasure - the right to request that we delete your personal information, subject to any legal obligations that require us to retain it.
  • Restriction - the right to request that we restrict how we use your information in certain circumstances.
  • Objection - the right to object to processing carried out on the basis of legitimate interest.
  • Portability - the right to receive personal information you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used format.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at advisory@hia.io. We will respond within one month. We do not charge for handling rights requests.

If you are not satisfied with how we have handled your personal information, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

08 Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. These include access controls, encrypted communication, and limiting the number of people with access to personal information.

No transmission over the internet is entirely secure. If you have concerns about sending sensitive information electronically, please contact us to discuss an alternative approach.

09 Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page indicates when it was last revised. Continued use of this website following any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

For questions about this policy or any aspect of how we handle your personal information, contact us at advisory@hia.io.
