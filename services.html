Independent Intervention Advisory

The Offering

Four tiers of engagement. A five-stage pathway from discovery to enterprise capability. Every tier is scoped in writing, delivered by senior practitioners, and designed to produce a result the board can act on immediately.

01How we work with organisations

Governance programmes produce frameworks. HIA tests whether those frameworks can produce action. Most AI governance defines what should happen when a system behaves unexpectedly. It rarely determines whether the people responsible for acting on that framework can do so, under time pressure, with incomplete information, against authority structures that were not designed for this moment.

Our work is practitioner-led. Every assessment is conducted by advisors with direct operational experience in AI systems environments, not analysts evaluating your governance against a compliance checklist. We evaluate against operational reality. AGDA, HIA's proprietary diagnostic architecture, is the structured mechanism through which we expose the gap between declared control and actual intervention capability.

  • Every engagement begins with a focused, confidential discovery call to establish context and determine whether HIA is a fit for the situation.
  • A written scope follows, defining what will be assessed, how, and what the engagement will produce. No generic frameworks. No standardised outputs.
  • Assessments are conducted by senior advisors with direct operational experience. Findings are accurate, not reassuring.
  • Findings are presented directly to the board or executive team: clear, prioritised, and specific to the conditions of your organisation.
If you are looking for reassurance, we are not the right advisor. If you want a clear account of what is actually true about your organisation's intervention capability and are prepared to act on it, we may work well together.
02The offering

Four tiers of engagement. Each begins where the previous one leaves your organisation stronger. Contained, scoped in writing, with clear outcomes.

I

Engagement scope
  • 4 to 8 weeks
  • Direct board and executive access required
  • Remote or in-person
  • Scoped in writing before work begins

The starting point. A board that does not know its own exposure cannot govern what it cannot see.

03How we select use cases

We focus on the systems that matter most, under the conditions where organisations are least likely to be able to stop them.

Selection criteria

Consequence velocity

Systems where failure produces regulatory, financial, or reputational consequences that compound by the hour. The faster the cost escalates, the more critical the intervention window.

Authority ambiguity

Use cases where the escalation path from system event to human authority is longest, most ambiguous, or spans multiple reporting lines that have never been tested under pressure.

Override gap

Decisions where the person with override authority cannot currently be reached, informed, and empowered to act within the window that matters.

Untested governance

Systems where the governance framework has never been tested against the operational conditions it was designed for. The framework exists. The proof that it works does not.

Not sure which use cases carry the highest intervention risk? The Exposure Assessment (Tier I) is designed to answer that question. Request a discovery call
