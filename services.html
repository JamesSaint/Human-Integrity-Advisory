01 How we work with organisations

HIA exists for the space that governance design cannot reach. Most AI governance programmes produce frameworks that define what should happen when a system behaves unexpectedly. They rarely determine whether the people responsible for acting on those frameworks can do so — under time pressure, with incomplete information, and against authority structures that were not designed with this moment in mind.

Our work is practitioner-led. Every assessment is conducted by advisors with direct operational experience in AI systems environments — not analysts evaluating your governance against a compliance checklist. We evaluate against operational reality.

If you are looking for reassurance, we are not the right advisor. If you want a clear account of what is actually true about your organisation's intervention capability — and are prepared to act on it — we may work well together.

03 What each service addresses

Each service is built around a specific question. What it is, why it matters, and what changes as a result.

Boardroom

What it is

Ongoing or project-based advisory to boards and executive leadership. A structured examination of whether the board can make effective decisions in the event of an AI system failure or governance challenge.

Why it matters — Most board-level AI governance provides assurance. It is rarely designed to ensure the board can act decisively when assurance is no longer adequate.

What changes — Boards gain structured clarity on authority, required information, and the conditions that would trigger an intervention. Ambiguity is replaced with a clear, testable framework.

Discuss this service
Architecture

What it is

A deep examination of the decision architecture surrounding your AI-dependent systems. HIA traces the chain from system event to human response — and identifies every point at which the chain can fail.

Why it matters — AI governance policies define what should happen when a system behaves unexpectedly. They almost never define who has authority to act, what information they need, or how quickly they can realistically respond.

What changes — Structural gaps become visible and addressable. A prioritised map of where authority is unclear, escalation paths are broken, or override speed does not match operational urgency.

Discuss this service
Labs

What it is

Structured, facilitated sessions with executive leadership, designed to expose genuine gaps in intervention capability through realistic AI system scenarios — not document reviews or hypotheticals.

Why it matters — You cannot simulate operational pressure in a document review. Most organisations discover their real intervention capability during a system event. These Labs surface that discovery first.

What changes — Participants gain direct, experience-based understanding of where decision-making fails under pressure — and a concrete account of what would need to change.

Discuss this service

Scope and clarity — Every engagement begins with a written scope defining responsibilities, communication channels, and boundaries. Assessments are contained and specific. There is no informal support or unscoped advisory outside an agreed engagement.

04 Choosing the right service

The right service depends on the specific question you are holding.

Board accountability

When you need to know whether your board can act — not just oversee

Boardroom Integrity Advisory is appropriate when the question is one of decision authority, information adequacy, and the gap between what governance is designed to deliver and what it can deliver under pressure.

Operational structure

When escalation and override are unclear or untested

The Intervention & Decision Architecture Review maps the actual decision architecture — not the documented one — and identifies where structural failure would prevent timely intervention.

Leadership readiness

When you want to know before an event reveals it

Executive Intervention Labs are appropriate when leadership needs a direct, experience-based understanding of whether they can act under realistic conditions — not a theoretical account of whether they should be able to.

05 Engagement model
  • Every engagement begins with a focused, confidential conversation to establish context and determine whether HIA is a fit for the situation.
  • A written scope follows, defining what will be assessed, how, and what the engagement will produce. No generic frameworks, no standardised outputs.
  • Assessments are conducted by senior advisors with direct operational experience. Findings are accurate, not reassuring.
  • Findings are presented directly to the board or executive team — clear, prioritised, and specific to the conditions of your organisation.

HIA works with a deliberately limited number of organisations. Engagements are not volume work. They are precision assessments that require and receive full senior attention.
