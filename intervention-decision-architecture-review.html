01The question this service answers

Where does your escalation and override architecture fail, and would you find out before a system event does?

AI governance policies define what should happen when a system behaves unexpectedly. They almost never define who has authority to act, what information they need, or how quickly they can realistically respond.

This review maps the actual decision architecture and identifies every point at which the chain can fail.

What governance policy cannot produce

  • Policy defines intent. It cannot confirm that override authority is clear, held by the right people, and exercisable at operational speed.
  • Documented escalation paths and functional escalation paths are not the same thing.
  • Structural gaps in decision architecture are invisible until a system event makes them visible. By then, the cost of discovery is not containable.
02What it is

A deep examination of the decision architecture surrounding your AI-dependent systems. HIA traces the chain from system event to human response and identifies every point at which the chain can fail. Practitioner-led. Evaluated against operational reality, not documentation standards. 03Why it matters

How the failure compounds

When an AI-driven decision produces outputs outside expected parameters, the system continues to run. The crisis begins at the moment of failure, not the moment of discovery. Every hour between those two points is an hour of accumulated cost.

The escalation chain determines how much of that time is recoverable. If the chain has structural gaps, authority that cannot be exercised at the required speed, or information that does not reach the right people, the window for low-cost intervention closes before the board is aware there is a problem.

What this review addresses

  • Whether escalation paths function under real operational conditions, not just on paper.
  • Whether override authority is held by the right people and can be exercised at operational speed.
  • Whether decision latency in your current architecture would prevent timely intervention.
  • Where structural gaps sit and how to address them in priority order.
Most organisations score well on governance design. They score poorly on operational intervention capability. This review addresses the second.
04What changes as a result

After the review

  • Structural gaps become visible and addressable before a system event makes them unavoidable.
  • A prioritised map of where authority is unclear, escalation paths are broken, or override speed does not match operational urgency.
  • Decision rights are documented against operational reality, not governance aspiration.
  • Remediation is sequenced by impact, not complexity.

How the review works

We begin by mapping the escalation chain as it currently exists, not as it is documented. Most organisations identify critical gaps in the first conversation. The full review traces the chain in detail across formal and informal paths, authority structures, and information flows.

Output

Findings are presented directly to the accountable executive. A written scope defines responsibilities, communication channels, and boundaries before work begins.
